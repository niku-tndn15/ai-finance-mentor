# UrPaisa Master Product PRD

## 1. Product Vision

UrPaisa is an AI-native personal finance mentor that helps users build better money habits over time. It is not just an expense tracker or budgeting app. Instead of only showing where money went, UrPaisa guides users on what to do next.

The product helps users understand:

- How much money is safe to spend.
- What financial risks they should watch.
- Which habits are hurting or helping progress.
- What small action they should take today.
- How to move closer to goals without feeling overwhelmed.

Core loop:

`Know your money situation -> get one clear action -> act on it -> build better habits -> improve over time`

## 2. Core User Problem

Most people know they should save, budget, reduce debt, and invest. The problem is not lack of information. The real problem is execution.

Users struggle with:

- Knowing how much they can safely spend.
- Remembering bills, EMIs, subscriptions, and goals.
- Making tradeoffs between spending today and saving for the future.
- Staying consistent after the first few weeks.
- Understanding which habit is hurting their financial progress.
- Translating generic financial advice into their own situation.

Existing finance apps are mostly passive. They show charts, categories, and past transactions. UrPaisa should be active: it behaves like a mentor that helps users make better financial decisions before and during spending.

## 3. Target User

Initial target users:

- Young working professionals.
- Students with income.
- Early-career employees.
- Gig workers and freelancers.
- People with monthly salary or recurring income who struggle with savings discipline.

Why this segment:

- They have recurring income or semi-recurring income.
- They have clear goals like emergency fund, rent, travel, education loan, investing, or debt repayment.
- They are comfortable with AI assistants.
- They need guidance more than complex dashboards.
- Their money habits are still forming.

## 4. Product Positioning

UrPaisa is positioned as:

**An AI money mentor that helps users build healthy financial habits through small personalized actions.**

UrPaisa is not:

- A full accounting tool.
- A tax product.
- A traditional budgeting spreadsheet.
- A generic financial education app.
- A passive expense tracker.

Product promise:

**UrPaisa helps you know what is safe to spend, what to protect, and what small money move to make next.**

## 5. Competitive Advantage

Most finance apps answer:

`Where did my money go?`

UrPaisa answers:

`What should I do next?`

Traditional apps are backward-looking. UrPaisa is forward-looking.

Traditional apps show categories. UrPaisa recommends actions.

Traditional apps need user discipline. UrPaisa builds discipline gradually.

Traditional apps track money. UrPaisa changes money behavior.

## 6. MVP Scope

The first version should prove whether users want an AI money coach that gives actions.

MVP features:

- Quick onboarding.
- Income and expense input.
- Safe-to-use money calculation.
- Goal setup.
- Simple debt input.
- Daily money nudge.
- Done, Skip, Remind me later buttons.
- Weekly reflection.
- Basic learning from user actions.
- Conversational money questions.

MVP should not include:

- Bank account integration.
- Automatic transaction categorization.
- Full accounting-style expense tracker.
- Tax guidance.
- Product-selling flows.
- Investment recommendations.
- Complex dashboard-first analytics.

## 7. Advanced Product Direction

After MVP validation, UrPaisa can expand into:

- Bank account integration.
- Automatic transaction categorization.
- Subscription detection.
- Salary day autopilot.
- Credit card due date tracking.
- AI Financial Twin simulations.
- Emergency mode.
- Purchase decision assistant.
- Habit challenges.
- Monthly reset.
- Family finance mode.
- Investment readiness guidance.
- Risk alerts.
- Personalized coaching tone.
- Money Health Score.

## 8. What Makes UrPaisa AI Native

UrPaisa is AI-native because AI is not just a chatbot layer. AI helps understand context, recommend actions, explain tradeoffs, adapt behavior, and coach the user over time.

AI is used for:

- Understanding user goals in natural language.
- Translating financial data into simple advice.
- Choosing the next best action.
- Adapting nudge timing and tone.
- Simulating financial decisions.
- Detecting habit patterns.
- Explaining tradeoffs in simple language.
- Creating personalized weekly reflections.
- Helping users make spending decisions.
- Coaching users through life changes.

## 9. Success Metrics

Activation:

- Percentage of users who complete onboarding.
- Percentage of users who set at least one goal.
- Percentage of users who view safe-to-use money.
- Percentage of users who complete first nudge.

Engagement:

- Weekly active users.
- Nudge completion rate.
- Number of money questions asked.
- Weekly reflection open rate.
- Challenge participation rate.

Behavior change:

- Increase in savings consistency.
- Reduction in missed bill payments.
- Reduction in unsafe spending days.
- Increase in goal contribution rate.
- Debt payment improvement.
- Emergency fund growth.

Retention:

- Week 1 retention.
- Week 4 retention.
- Month 3 retention.
- Percentage of users who update goals.
- Percentage of users who continue using weekly reflection.
